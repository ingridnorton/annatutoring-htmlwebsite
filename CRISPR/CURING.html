CURING WITH CRISPR

To cure a disease using CRISPR, you’ve got to start with RNA. RNA is a molecule that can read the genetic code in DNA. The RNA finds the spot in the nucleus of a cell where some gene editing activity should take place and guides the Cas9 to that location. The Cas9 enzyme then makes the cut, and leaves the cell to repair itself.

When your cell fixes a break, it does so by glueing them back together, it isn’t the cleanest process, but it is effective nonetheless. During this process, there is a chance that it might disable the remainder of the targeted gene in the process.

     

CURING CYSTIC FIBROSIS

 

Cystic Fibrosis (CF) is a genetic disease that primarily affects the lungs and digestive system because of a malfunction in the exocrine glands. For someone to have CF, they must have inherited two copies of a defective CFTR gene, one copy from each parent. CF is where your body produces a huge amount of really sticky mucus within your airways, lungs and digestive system. Ultimately leading to irreversible damage.

 

CRISPR/Cas9 is an experimental approach for treating CF. A new variation of CRISPR can correct mutations in the CFTR gene which is the genetic cause of cystic fibrosis in the stem cells of CF patients.

 

Although there isn’t a known cure, the research to cure CF could save thousands of lives.
