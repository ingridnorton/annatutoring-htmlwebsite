WHAT IS THE CAS9 PROTEIN?

The Cas9 Protein, CAS9 standing for CRISPR Associated Protein 9, is a protein that cuts DNA. The CAS9 protein is also called “molecular scissors” and cuts out the mutated gene at the cleavage site. A new gene sequence without the genetic mutation is inserted.

WHAT IS RNA?

RNA is short for Ribonucleic acid. It does many things but its role in CRISPR CAS9 is acting as a guide to help the CAS9 protein find the problematic DNA, so that it can fix the mutated gene. It binds with various strands of DNA, checking to see if they match a certain sequence.

WHAT IS PAM?

PAM stands for Protospacer Adjacent Motif. PAM is a motif that occurs every 50 bases or so. This explains why we can edit almost any human gene. CAS9 binds to this sequence so that it can check the DNA next to it and see if it matches to a certain DNA strand.
