WHAT ARE THE IMPACTS OF CRISPR?

ADVANTAGES

  • This process is efficient, simple and cheap

    •  

  • can be applied directly in embryo, CRISPR-CAS9 reduces the time required to modify target genes compared to other gene targeting technologies based on the use of embryonic stem (ES) cells.
    •  
  • May prevent and treat diseases particularly hereditary ones.

 

DRAWBACKS

  • Some aspects (such as non-homologous repair) are unreliable. It increases the risk of mutation in the gene sequence. The effect of an off-target alteration can alter the function of a gene and may result in genomic instability, hindering it prospective and application in clinical procedure.

    •  

  • CRISPR- CAS9 in embryos works extremely well for the generation of simple alleles (each of two or more alternative forms of a gene that arise by mutation and are found at the same place on a chromosome) such as constitutive knockout and knock in of point mutations, but is not the best technology for the introduction of more complex modifications relying on homologous recombination over larger regions.

 

IS EDITING GENES ETHICAL?

CRISPR technology has raised multiple ethical questions about the consequences of tampering with human genes. Editing our genomes has a potential ecological impact (the effects of human actions and natural occurrences on organisms and their environment).

  • Introduced traits could spread beyond the targeted population through crossbreeding. This reduces genetic diversity.

    •  

  • Genome editing (making genetic modifications to human embryos and reproductive cells) raises concerns as these changes ca be passed down to future generations.

 

However, theres is also the argument that one day not editing your child's genes could be unethical. By not editing their genes would be condemning them to a life with a disease that is easily preventable.

 

Since our knowledge on human genetics, gene-environment interactions and diseases is ever growing and still fairly limited, our actions could cause unintended consequences for our future generations.
